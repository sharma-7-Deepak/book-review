  Review 1: A Gripping Sci-Fi Adventure

     "The Last Watch" by J.S. Dewes is an exhilarating science fiction novel that captivates from the first page.
     Set on the edge of the universe, the story follows a group of soldiers stationed on the Argus, a space station tasked 
     with monitoring the boundary of known space. The characters are well-developed, each bringing a unique perspective and 
     depth to the narrative. Dewes masterfully blends high-stakes action with moments of introspection, creating a balanced 
     and engaging read. The plot twists kept me on the edge of my seat, and the intricate world-building made the setting 
     feel incredibly real. This book is a must-read for any sci-fi enthusiast.
                
     Pros:
                
     Intriguing and well-developed characters
     Intense and suspenseful plot
     Rich world-building
     Cons:
                
     Some pacing issues in the middle chapters
            
  Review 2: A Stellar Debut

     J.S. Dewes' debut novel, "The Last Watch," is a fantastic addition to the science fiction genre. The story centers 
     around the Sentinels, soldiers who are essentially the galaxy's last line of defense. The narrative is fast-paced
     filled with unexpected twists and turns. Dewes' writing is sharp and immersive, making it easy to get lost in the 
     vastness of space and the intricate dynamics of the crew. The book explores themes of duty, loyalty, and survival   
     refreshing and thought-provoking manner. It's a thrilling ride from start to finish, and I highly recommend it to 
     fans of space operas and military sci-fi.
                
  Pros:
                
     Fast-paced and engaging storyline
     Thought-provoking themes
     Vivid and immersive writing
  Cons:
                
     Some secondary characters could have been more fleshed out
